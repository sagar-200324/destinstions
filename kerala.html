  • Location : The temple is located in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, by the side of the Padma Theertham tank.
  • History : The temple dates back to the 8th century, but the present structure was built in the 18th century.
  • Architecture : The temple is built in the Chera style, which is unique to Kerala and the neighboring states. The temple's design is suited to the local weather conditions.
  • Deity : The main deity of the temple is Lord Vishnu. The central deity is made up of 12,008 saligrams, which are fossilized shells from the Gandaki River in Nepal.
  • Treasure : The temple is one of India's costliest temples, with an estimated value of over Rs 1,00,000 crore in gold, gems, and statues. The temple's vaults were opened in 2011 after a Public Interest Litigation was filed by a local activist.
  • Features : The temple has a 100-foot high, seven-tiered gopuram built in the Pandyan style. It also has a corridor with 365 and one-quarter sculptured granite-stone pillars.
  • Hours : The temple is open every day from 3:30 AM to 12 PM and from 5 PM to 7:20 PM.
