TELANGANA

  • Construction : The Charminar was built in 1591 using granite and lime mortar. It's known for its blend of Indo-Islamic and Persian architecture.
  • Design : The Charminar is a square structure with four minarets, each 48.7 meters high. The minarets are supported by four grand arches, and each has a dome-shaped roof. The Charminar's roof has a large platform with panoramic views of the city.
  • History : The Charminar's construction is surrounded by several theories, including that it was built to celebrate Hyderabad's freedom from plague, to mark the start of the Islamic millennium, or as the main mosque in the city.
  • Significance : The Charminar is a symbol of Hyderabad and is featured in the emblem of Telangana. It's also a popular tourist attraction and a site for festivals like Eid-ul-adha and Eid al-Fitr.
  • Location : The Charminar is located on the east bank of the Musi River, next to the Makkah Masjid and the Laad Bazaar.
  • Nearby attractions : The Charminar is surrounded by popular local markets.
  • History : The fort's history dates back to the 11th century when it was built by the Kakatiya dynasty as a mud fort. It became the capital of the Qutb Shahi dynasty in the 16th century and was a center of power and trade. In the late 17th century, the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb conquered the fort and annexed it to the Mughal Empire.
  • Architecture : The fort is known for its unique acoustic architecture, including the Fateh Darwaza, or Victory Gate, where clapping near the dome entrance can be heard at the hill top pavilion.
  • Location : The fort is located on a granite hill 120 meters high on the western outskirts of Hyderabad.
  • Features : The fort has eight gateways, four drawbridges, mounted cannons, and majestic halls, magazines, and stables. The inner fort contains ruins of palaces, mosques, and a hill top pavilion.
  • Known for : The region was known for its diamonds, including the world-famous Koh-i-noor and Great Mogul diamonds.
  • Status : The fort is currently abandoned and in ruins. In 2014, UNESCO put the complex on its "tentative list" to become a World Heritage Site
